Frequently Asked Questions

Does HarvardTax replace Drake?

No. HarvardTax sits between QuickBooks and Drake. We handle the mapping and allocation grunt work, then export a trial balance file that imports directly into Drake. You still review and e-file in Drake as usual.

What if a QBO account doesn't map correctly?

Every mapping is visible and overridable. If an account should go to a different 1065 line, change it with one click and regenerate. Your professional judgment always wins.

Is my client's data secure?

We use read-only QBO access via OAuth2 — we never see your client's QBO password. Data is encrypted in transit and at rest. SSNs are used only for K-1 generation and not stored permanently.

What types of partnerships are supported?

LLCs, LPs, LLPs, and general partnerships. We handle pro-rata allocations now, with waterfall, preferred return, and carried interest support coming soon.

Do I need Drake specifically?

Drake is our first integration — it's the most popular tax software among small and mid-size CPA firms. Lacerte and CCH ProSystem support are on our roadmap.

What about complex allocations?

We currently support pro-rata allocations based on ownership percentages. Tiered allocations, waterfalls, and 704(b) special allocations are on the roadmap — these returns still benefit from the QBO mapping and Drake export even with manual allocation adjustments.
